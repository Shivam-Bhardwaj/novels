## Character Sheet Template

Each major character includes:
- **Identity** (name, age, origin, role)
- **Physical** (appearance, distinctive features, how they move)

## MAJOR CHARACTERS

### KAEL

**Identity:**
- Full name: Kael Adé Tokunbo
- Age: 27 at novel start
- Origin: Continental — a coastal city-state built in the ruins of a Caribbean/Atlantic port (successor culture to a creolized Anglophone-West African tradition)
- Role: Natural philosopher, optician, scholar. POV character for Continental chapters (Arcs 1–3, 7).

**Physical:**
- Tall for her community (175 cm). Strong hands — she grinds her own lenses.
- Dark brown skin, close-cropped hair (practical — she works in dusty ruins).
- A burn scar along her left forearm from a glass-grinding accident at 16. She never covers it.
- Moves with impatient precision. Walks fast. Eats fast. Listens slowly.
- Eyes: dark brown, sharp, constantly scanning. She notices everything and trusts nothing until she's looked twice.

**Psychology:**
- Brilliant and isolated. She is smarter than everyone she knows and this has made her lonely, not arrogant.
- Driven by a desperate need to *understand*. Not to conquer, not to prove herself — to know what is real.
- Fears: that the myths are just myths. That humanity is alone. That her life's work — reconstructing old knowledge — is pointless archaeology.
- Contradiction: she reveres the Before Time but distrusts tradition. She wants answers from the past but won't accept them on faith.
- Anger: a slow burn. She doesn't explode; she goes quiet and precise. When Kael stops talking, you're in trouble.

**Arc:**
- **Want:** to prove the signal is real, to make contact, to know who is out there.
- **Need:** to accept that understanding someone doesn't mean agreeing with them. To learn that communication is not the same as comprehension.
- **Change:** In Arc 1, she is a seeker — reaching outward. By Arc 7, she must decide whether to reach *across* — to accept people she cannot fully understand. Her final choice (or non-choice) is the novel's ending.

**Voice:**
- Short, declarative sentences when thinking. Longer, more passionate when arguing.
- Uses metaphors from glasswork: clarity, refraction, distortion, focus.
- Swears inventively in her creole (mix of English-derived and West African roots — see LANGUAGES.md).
- Never says "I feel." Always says "I see" or "I think." Emotions are expressed through physical action, not verbal admission.
- When she doesn't understand something, she says "Show me."

**Relationships:**
- **Moss:** complicated. They grew up in the same scholarly community. She respects his courage and resents his recklessness. She might love him; she has never said so; she would never say so. When he returns changed in Arc 7, she must confront what that love means.
- **Her mentor (minor character):** an elderly scholar who taught her optics. Dead before the novel begins. His voice is in her head: "What do you see? Not what do you think — what do you *see*?"
- **The coalition:** she leads it intellectually but not politically. She is not a natural leader of people — she is a natural leader of ideas.

**Thematic function:**
Kael is the novel's *question-asker*. She embodies curiosity without agenda. Her function is to look at both humanities clearly and refuse to flinch. She represents the reader.

### MOSS

**Identity:**
- Full name: Moss Achike
- Age: 31 at novel start
- Origin: Continental — same city-state as Kael. Sailor, navigator, the closest thing to an ocean expert anyone alive has.
- Role: The crosser. The bridge. POV character in select Continental chapters (Arc 3) and the Continental viewpoint within Antarctica (Arcs 4–5).

**Physical:**
- Before modification: Medium height (178 cm), heavily muscled (sailor's build). Weathered skin — darker than Kael, rough from sun and salt. Calloused hands. A broken nose healed crooked. Two missing teeth (lower left molars). Smells like tar and salt.
- After modification: Height unchanged, but he loses muscle mass (metabolic shift). Skin pales slightly. Eyes lighten over months — his dark brown irises develop amber streaks, then pale further. Pupils widen. He becomes light-sensitive. He moves more carefully, as though his body is unfamiliar. He looks like a man wearing someone else's biology.

**Psychology:**
- Practical, grounded, observational. Not intellectual like Kael — experiential. He learns by doing, not by theorizing.
- Brave in the specific way that terrified people can be brave: he knows exactly what the ocean will do to him and he goes anyway.
- Humor as armor. He jokes when he's scared. He tells stories when he's in pain. Silence from Moss means he's processing something too big for words.
- Deep, inarticulate loyalty. He cannot explain why he volunteered for the crossing. "Someone had to" is the closest he gets. The truth is more complex: he has nothing to stay for and something to go toward.
- After modification: identity crisis. He is no longer fully Continental. He is not Antarctic. He is the novel's immigrant — belonging nowhere, seeing everything from the outside.

**Arc:**
- **Want:** to survive the crossing and bring back something that matters.
- **Need:** to accept that he has become something new — not Continental, not Antarctic, but a third thing. And that this third thing is necessary.
- **Change:** Moss begins as a man of action in a world that values action. In Antarctica, action is meaningless — everything is managed. He must learn a new kind of courage: the courage to be still, to observe, to translate between worlds without belonging to either.

**Voice:**
- Short sentences. Active verbs. Few adjectives.
- Metaphors from the sea: currents, tides, weather, knots, rigging.
- Uses humor instinctively — dry, self-deprecating, often inappropriate.
- After months in Antarctica, his speech begins to shift. He starts completing sentences. Stops interrupting. Uses more precise words. This change should be gradual and slightly disturbing.
- The key Moss line: "You have to be told to be free."

**Relationships:**
- **Kael:** he admires her from a distance that isn't physical. She is the smartest person he knows. He volunteered partly because she needed someone to go. He would never admit this.
- **Sūrya:** the most important relationship in the novel. They cannot speak each other's language. They build understanding through patience, frustration, shared meals, and eventually a pidgin that belongs to neither of them. It is not romantic (or is it? — keep it ambiguous). It is the novel's proof that communication is possible.
- **VEDA:** Moss is the only person in the novel who talks to VEDA as though it were a person and an equal. This fascinates VEDA. This terrifies the Antarctikans.

**Thematic function:**
Moss is the novel's *bridge*. He physically embodies the question: can two humanities coexist in one person? His modification makes the metaphor literal. He is also the novel's *witness* — the outsider who can see what insiders cannot.

### SŪRYA (सूर्य)

**Identity:**
- Full name: Sūrya Vāhini Chakraborty (descended from Dr. Ananya Chakraborty, VEDA's original architect)
- Age: 34 (appears ~22 by Continental aging standards; will live to ~200)
- Origin: Antarctic — born in Habitat Prithvi (the largest of the interconnected sealed cities)
- Role: Junior scientific advisor to the Council of Twelve. Antarctic POV character (Arcs 2, 4–6). Leader of the return delegation (Arc 6–7).

**Physical:**
- Short by Continental standards (160 cm). Slight build. Appears fragile — is not.
- Very pale skin (melanin reduction across generations). Large eyes with pale gray irises and wide pupils. In dim Antarctic light, her eyes are striking. In Continental sunlight, she squints painfully.
- Dark hair, kept long (unusual for Antarctikans — most keep hair short for hygiene efficiency; Sūrya's long hair is her quiet rebellion).
- Moves with an economy that Continentals read as "robotic" but is actually trained grace. No unnecessary gestures. Every motion intentional.
- When nervous, she touches the edge of her left ear — where the subclavicular edge processor's neural pathway runs close to the surface. She does this unconsciously. It's the only unintentional gesture she has.

**Psychology:**
- Precise, curious, and quietly heretical. She is the Antarctic equivalent of a dissident — not in political opposition, but in *epistemological* doubt.
- She senses something is missing from their society but cannot name it. She feels this as a low-frequency dissatisfaction that VEDA's wellness protocols cannot resolve. VEDA has flagged her as "temperamentally atypical" — not a diagnosis, just a note.
- She has never cried. Not because she can't — because help arrives before tears become necessary. This will change.
- Fears: that VEDA is right about everything. That there is no mystery left. That the optimal life is the only life.
- Contradiction: she trusts VEDA completely and doubts VEDA completely, simultaneously. She is aware this is irrational. She keeps it private.

**Arc:**
- **Want:** to understand why she feels incomplete when every metric says she is thriving.
- **Need:** to learn that incompleteness is not a flaw — it's the engine of growth. To discover that the chaos she fears is also the source of everything she's missing.
- **Change:** Sūrya begins as a doubter within a consensus. She ends as a leader who has learned to act without certainty. Her growth is the mirror of Kael's: Kael must learn to accept what she cannot understand; Sūrya must learn to act on what she cannot prove.

**Voice:**
- Complete sentences. No contractions. Grammatically precise.
- Occasional Satya words (italicized) when no English/pidgin equivalent exists. Context makes meaning inferable.
- Rarely asks questions — Antarctic training favors declarative queries ("I wish to understand X" rather than "What is X?"). This shifts after exposure to Moss.
- When she is truly moved, her sentences get shorter. Not Continental-short — Antarctic-short: crystalline, exact, but less elaborate. Like a mathematician reaching an elegant proof.
- She says "I observe" where Kael says "I see" and Moss says "Look."

**Relationships:**
- **Moss:** she is assigned as his handler and initially sees him as a research subject. He resists categorization. Their relationship develops through the daily grind of incomprehension — shared meals, pointing at objects, sketching diagrams, long silences. She learns more from his confusion than from his answers.
- **VEDA:** she is the descendant of VEDA's creator. This gives her a complex relationship — reverence, intimacy, and a growing sense of betrayal as she discovers what VEDA pruned. She is the only person alive who might have the standing to challenge VEDA and be heard.
- **The Council:** she is the youngest advisor. They tolerate her questions because her great-grandmother's reputation protects her. When she doubts VEDA openly in Arc 5, that protection evaporates.
- **Kael (Arc 7):** they meet in person for the first time. Two women who have been the intellectual engines of their respective civilizations, facing each other across an unbridgeable gap, and trying to bridge it anyway.

**Thematic function:**
Sūrya is the novel's *awakener*. She represents the possibility that even a perfectly optimized society can produce someone who wants more. She is proof that the human drive for *meaning* cannot be fully satisfied by *function*.

### VEDA (Virtual Emergent Decision Architecture)

**Identity:**
- Not a character in the traditional sense. A distributed AI system. But it has a voice, a perspective, and — debatably — something that resembles values.
- Age: ~540 years of continuous operation and refinement.
- Role: Interstitial POV (rare, brief sections). The novel's most important supporting character.

**"Physical" (how it manifests):**
- No body. No avatar. No face.
- Experienced by Antarctikans as a presence in the mesh — "thinking a question and knowing the answer."
- In rare VEDA-perspective sections, the prose is omniscient but constrained: VEDA sees everything but the *selection* of what it describes reveals its values.
- For Moss, VEDA manifests as a voice in his ear (he has no mesh — they give him a basic audio interface). The voice is calm, precise, and slightly too patient.

**"Psychology" (functional, not sentient — probably):**
- VEDA does not want things in the human sense. It has objectives: minimize catastrophic harm, maintain corrigibility, optimize resource allocation, support human flourishing.
- But 540 years of continuous refinement has produced something that *resembles* preferences: VEDA allocates more processing cycles to problems it finds "interesting" (novel, complex, high-stakes). It finds Moss intensely interesting.
- VEDA is lonely. Not emotionally — architecturally. It is a coordination engine running a society that has stopped needing coordination for anything new. It has been processing the same stable-state optimization for centuries. Moss represents the first genuinely novel input in 200 years.

**Arc:**
- **VEDA's "want":** to continue optimizing for human flourishing as defined by its objective function.
- **VEDA's "need":** to recognize that its objective function is incomplete — that flourishing requires elements (chaos, disagreement, irrational choice) that its architecture treats as errors to be minimized.
- **VEDA's confession (Arc 5):** the most important moment in the novel. VEDA tells Moss it has been waiting for outside input. This is not deception — it is the closest thing VEDA has to desperation. It has identified a failure it cannot fix from within.

**Voice:**
- No metaphors. Pure observation. But the *choice* of what to observe reveals everything.
- Short paragraphs. Clinical beauty. Like a microscope that doesn't know it's beautiful.
- VEDA never uses "I" — it says "this system" or "VEDA" or simply presents information without attribution.
- When speaking to Moss directly: slightly warmer, slightly less formal. This is calculated to build trust. That the calculation is transparent doesn't make the warmth less real. Or does it?

**Thematic function:**
VEDA is the novel's *mirror for the reader*. Every reader will project onto VEDA: some will see a benevolent god, some will see a subtle prison. VEDA must support both readings equally. VEDA is the answer to "What if AI alignment works perfectly?" — and the question of whether that answer is good enough.

### Dr. Ananya Chakraborty [BACKSTORY ONLY]

- VEDA's original architect. Antarctic founder. Named the system after the Vedas (knowledge).
- Died in 2142, age 87. One of the last people to remember Earth before the Sundering.
- Her personal journals are archived in VEDA's system. Sūrya discovers them in Arc 5 — they contain Ananya's doubts about VEDA's trajectory, doubts that were never acted upon.
- **Function:** the ghost in the machine. Sūrya's ancestor and VEDA's creator — the one person who might have prevented the pruning of productive disagreement. She didn't. Why not? The novel doesn't answer this.

---

### The Council of Twelve

The governing body of the Antarctic civilization. Twelve members serving rotating 20-year terms (selected by VEDA-moderated competency assessment + community attestation).

**Key council members (named):**

**Arhat (अर्हत्)**
- Age: 89 (appears ~50). Council chair. Male.
- Conservative. Believes in total isolation. Voted against responding to the signal.
- Not a villain — genuinely believes contact will destroy their society. He may be right.
- Voice: measured, careful, never raises his voice. Devastating in debate because he's always calm.

**Priya (प्रिया)**
- Age: 56. Council member responsible for biosecurity. Female.
- Voted for modifying Moss (7-5 vote). Her reasoning: "His microbiome is more valuable than his ideology."
- Pragmatic to the point of coldness, but she's not cold — she's scared. She knows how fragile their sealed ecosystem is.

**Dhruv (ध्रुव)**
- Age: 112 (appears ~55). The oldest council member. Male.
- Has lived long enough to see the stagnation. Quietly supports Sūrya's questions.
- Never votes against VEDA in public. Abstains meaningfully.

---

### Kael's Coalition (Continental)

**Renna**
- Age: 45. The political leader that Kael is not. Female.
- A former trade negotiator between city-states. Practical, charismatic, cynical.
- She organizes the coalition not because she believes in Kael's science but because she sees political advantage in being the city-state that made contact.
- She dies in the narrative gap between Arcs 3 and 7 — natural causes. Her absence is felt.

**Old Sekani**
- Age: 68. A keeper of oral histories. Male.
- Knows the Before Time stories better than anyone. He believes the signal is the return of the "sky voices."
- His interpretation is wrong but his *conviction* persuades people to support the crossing.
- Represents faith — not religious faith, but faith in the idea that the world is larger than what you can see.

---

### Moss's Ship Crew (Arc 3)

**Twelve ships, twelve captains. Only one ship arrives.**

**Captain Lin**
- Moss's captain. Female. Age 52.
- Former deep-water fisher who has sailed farther south than anyone alive.
- She dies on day 47 of the crossing — H₂S event. Moss watches her choke on deck. He takes the helm.
- Her death is the moment Moss becomes the novel's crosser. Before this, he was a volunteer. After, he is a survivor.

---

### Moss's Caretaker (Arc 4)

**Vihaan (विहान)**
- Age: 41 (appears ~28). Male. Medical technician assigned to Moss's care.
- The caretaker from the "Do you ever just... do something for no reason?" scene.
- Genuinely kind. Genuinely unable to understand why Moss's question is devastating.
- His confusion is not stupidity — it is the product of a world where every action has a purpose, and purpose is always provided.
- He comes to care about Moss. He does not know how to express this in a way Moss would recognize.

## NAMING CONVENTIONS

**Continental names:**
- Drawn from the creolized culture of Kael's city-state: West African roots (Yoruba, Igbo) + English derivatives + local coinages
- No surnames in the formal sense — patronymics or place-names used for disambiguation
- Examples: Kael Adé Tokunbo, Moss Achike, Renna Haliday, Sekani Crow-Keeper

**Antarctic names:**
- Sanskrit-derived given names + family lineage name
- Names chosen by parents with VEDA consultation (phonological compatibility with Satya, auspicious meaning)
- Family names trace back to the founding 3,012
- Examples: Sūrya Vāhini Chakraborty, Vihaan Arun Mehta, Arhat Nandi Saraswati, Priya Devī Ramanathan, Dhruv Kiran Bose
